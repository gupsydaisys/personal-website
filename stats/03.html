Face-to-face Group
The face-to-face group was all asked the question sometime in the last two weeks. For all the people, I
would not bring it up mid-conversation.
I would first ask them then chat with them if I chat at all with
them. The conversation would go as follows for everyone:
Serena:
“Hi, I’m Serena, a junior at Menlo.
For my stats class, we are taking surveys of people to
statistically analyze.
My project focuses on how survey type affects people’s answers to questions.
I
was wondering if you had a minute to answer my question.
If you choose to answer, know that I won’t
tell your name or answer to anyone.”
Other Person: “Ya, go for it” (Most typical response)
Serena: “Have you ever had sexual intercourse?”
Other Person: …
Serena: “Thanks for your time!”
Note: Unsurprisingly, I turned mildly pink asking most seniors and some other people, which probably
made them take me less seriously.
Also, the venue wasn’t the same for everyone.
I tried to ask the
question the same way, but sometimes I would seem more happy or sad or stressed out, which could
have affected the responses.
In the future, I probably would have asked like everyone at lunch or
something and tried to act similar each time.
Written Questionnaire Group
The written questionnaire group was all given the survey sometime in the last two weeks. My means
for handing it out were as follows.
The conversation would go as follows for everyone (even the
people who I already knew):
Serena:
“Hi, I’m Serena, a junior at Menlo.
For my stats class, we are taking surveys of people to
statistically analyze.
My project focuses on how survey type affects people’s answers to questions.
I
was wondering if you had a minute to take my survey. If you choose to answer, know that I won’t tell
your name or answer to anyone.”
Other Person: “Ya, sure” (Most typical response)
Other Person: (Scribble scribble scribble.
Then they hand it to me.)
Serena: “Thanks for your time!”
Note: As I said before with the face-to-face group, the venue varied from person to person.
I think that
less affected the answering this time but nonetheless could have.
I also forgot to provide pens for
everyone, which led to a bit of chaos but again, didn’t dramatically affect answering I don’t think.
