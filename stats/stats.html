Introduction
:
The topic I was interested in pertained to how the collection of data affected people’s answers
to controversial questions.
I choose it because for one of the units we spoke about different bias and
how they may affect data.
I wasn’t sure if certain questioning types really had a significant affect on
answers and thus wanted to find out.
I ended up asking people whether or not they have had sexual
intercourse.
In addition, I took down their gender and grade level at my previous high school Menlo.
I
asked this via Facebook chat, survey monkey, face-to-face, and written questionnaires.
I then used Chi
Square Goodness of Fit and 2 Proportion Z Tests to analyze my results.
Summary of Experiment:
For data collection, I randomly generated using my calculator a list of page numbers in the
Facebook and another list of numbers 1-12.
Using those numbers, I generated a list of 140 names by
going to that page and then finding the nth person on that page where n is defined by the 1-12 list.
Note if the person was in middle school or not at Menlo anymore, I would go to the next randomly
generated number.
The first 35 people were in Facebook chat group, next 35 in survey monkey, next
35 in face-to-face, and last 35 in written questionnaire.
Also, people had the option prior to me asking
the question to opt out of my survey, but everyone said yes.
